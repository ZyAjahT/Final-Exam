Overcrowding of Animal Shelters

How are Animal Shelters So Overcrowded?

Animal shelters all across the U.S. are overcrowded due to a variety of reasons. One reason is that family pets are often surrendered because they either get too old, ill, or they’re inconvenient or too expensive to take care of. Also, another big reason why animal shelters are so crowded is because owners put unrealistic expectations onto their pets. Poor understanding of normal pet behavior, improper training, and lack of socialization causes a lot of owners to surrender their pets.

Why Are They Struggling?

Animal shelters have been experiencing massive financial issues due to the overpopulation of animals. Many shelters have a lack of resources to accommodate the overflow of animals. According to USA Today, “it costs U.S. taxpayers approximately $2 billion each year to round up, house, kill and dispose of homeless animals.” And each year, costs keep increasing, making it almost impossible to care for all these animals.

How Can You Help?

You can help these shelters by donating money or resources. Whether it be at your local shelters or some place you found online, anything helps.
